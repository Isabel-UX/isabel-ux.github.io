Skyscanner App redesign

This proposal focuses on improving the experience of users who search for and purchase flights through the Skyscanner App. This report presents the sketches, wireframes and final high definition designs of three screens of the application for Android devices.



Skyscanner is an online user-driven search and travel management company, launched in 2002 and has evolved to include more services, features and better design patterns since then. Its App for Android and IOS was launched in 2011, and was last updated on March 9, 2020.


Thus, the importance of research and User Centred Design. A compendium of methodologies and ways of working that put the user in the centre of all phases of the design process, in which it participates or in which it is directly related.



Overall analysis


In general, the app works smoothly and has enough paths so that users can find what they are looking for and locate themselves within the navigation.
It is worth noting the functionalities and drop-down menus of the results screens and search areas. It has a powerful filtering and sorting of the results screen, a search drop-down list where recent searches are offered, and it offers access to the user's GPS location as a starting point for the search.
The pages that have been selected are the main page from which the search process starts, the explicit search screen and the results screen. In this case, although it would have been interesting to show the sorting filters and the search drop-down menus, the application does not show any potential problem in the existing ones.



1. Main search screen


Search by known item, by knowledge of the existence of an item and exploration.
The navigation on the main screen is based on several categories, search by known item, knowledge of the existence of an item and exploration. However, it is not too marked, Figure 1. In the redesign proposal, the essence of the application and functions is maintained while the categories are highlighted and some of them are renamed and reorganized, in order to facilitate the identification of the sections. In addition to modifying the aesthetic appearance of the interface.



2. Explicit search screen


Search by known item. The navigation in the explicit search screen is based on a simple form without many options or selectors. It is a preliminary search to which it will be possible to apply filters to sort the results. In the redesign proposal, the appearance of the screen is improved, expanding the query form area and making its interaction clearer. On the other hand, the search drop-down menu that the application currently has is adequate and no changes need to be made to it.




3. Results screen


Display and sort results. As mentioned above, the application's sorting filters are adequate and do not need to be redesigned, they are easy to understand and easy to use. However, the results screen could be improved by adding some new functions such as "like" to mark a flight as a favorite and make it easier to search for it later, improve the automatic positioning of the cheapest and shortest flights, and redistribute the share button and the create alerts button on routes and dates.
