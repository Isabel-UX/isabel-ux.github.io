UX design process

User experience can be defined as the set of factors and elements that generate a perception, positive or negative, in the user when an interaction occurs. In other words, user experience is a complex notion that is influenced by the characteristics of the product, the context of use that sourround the user, and the user's emotions.



When it comes to designing a solution it is important to consider that the product must respond to the complete user experience; as emotions affect user's attention, performance and memory capabilities. Not only must usability be taken into an account, but emotions must also be included in the process. These are of vital importance when it comes to interpreting and understanding products, as well as evoking positive memories in people.


The fact that people who built the site didn't care enough to make things obvious - and easy - can erode our confidence in the site and the organization behind it. Steve Krug

Thus, the importance of research and User Centred Design. A compendium of methodologies and ways of working that put the user in the centre of all phases of the design process, in which it participates or in which it is directly related.



Principles of User Centred Design

  • Design is based on explicit understanding of users, activities and environments.
  • Users are involved throughout the design process.
  • The design is driven and adjusted by user-centered evaluation.
  • The process is iterative The design responds to the complete user experience.
  • The design team includes multidisciplinary profiles and perspectives.
  • All difficulty estimating unreserved increasing the solicitude.

Phases of the design process


1. Planning.
2. Research. Understand adn define the context of use.
3. Definition. Design requirements.
4. Generate solutions.
5. Evaluation. Design review.
